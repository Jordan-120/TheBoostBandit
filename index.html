Cobalt SS/TC Description

Only 3,168 Cobalt SS Turbo coupes were ever manufactured. This makes Cobalt SS/TC one of the rarest vehicles every produced. This was GM's first foray into the tuner market. The Cobalt SS switched from supercharged LSJ to turbocharged LNF in 2007. Also adding 24 mm front stabilizer bar, 22 mm rear stabilizer bar, F35 tuned suspension and a limited slip differential. Due to this switch, the Chevrolet Cobalt SS/TC holds the record for the fastest lap time for a front-wheel drive production car at the NÃ¼rburgring Nordschleife, with a time of 8:22.85 minutes. This vehicle boasted extreme performance over appearance. The downside to this was the lack of appeal to the general consumers. Production then ended in 2010. These cars are favorited by racers today due to the low cost for track ready performance.
